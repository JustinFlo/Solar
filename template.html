Mercury

Starting at Mercury feels like remembering the moment I first admitted to myself that learning, especially in STEM. It starts with being confused. Mercury is hot, rushed, and close to the source, just like how I felt when I first tried to solve math problems that didn’t make sense or tried to understand loops in Python turtle. I was impatient, jumping too fast, the way Mercury speeds around the Sun. But in that heat, I learned my first truth: I don’t grow by knowing everything, I grow by being willing to start anyway.


Venus

I’ve had moments where concepts felt backwards, like why logs combine a certain way, or why moving a minus sign on an equation wasn’t as simple as subtracting something. Venus mirrors that confusion, covered in thick clouds that hide what’s beneath. When burnout crept in, or when I reread the same line in a PDF trying to understand self-compassion, Venus was the planet I stood on: surrounded by pressure, but still forcing myself to look for clarity. Here I learned that the fog isn’t failure; it’s part of the process of clearing my own path.


Earth

On Earth, things feel grounded. This is where I connected ideas: how a turtle loop actually repeats, why functions save time, why questioning the status quo in my writing matters, how policy proposals work, and how quiet acts of rebellion can shift entire systems. Earth is the part of my journey where I start understanding not just the answers but the reasons behind them. It’s where learning stops being something I chase and becomes something I build.


Mars

When I tried drawing shapes with the least lines possible or solving math problems that fooled me with their simplicity, Mars is where I landed. It’s dry, rough, unpredictable, just like trying to write a clean, simple Python script and getting errors that made no sense at first. But Mars is also the planet of persistence, and each mistake became a little rover-sized victory. Here, I learned that progress isn’t smooth, but rather it’s dusty, stubborn, and worth every misstep.


Jupiter

This is where I felt the heaviness of expectations: studying for tests, understanding networks like TCP/IP, managing burnout, and still trying to produce clean, clear writing. Jupiter’s storms mirror the internal storms I’ve had, especially when I tried to simplify complex topics without losing meaning. But Jupiter also taught me that storms don’t last forever. In the middle of pressure, I discovered strength I didn’t know I had.


Saturn

This is the part of my STEM journey where I started becoming more organized, where turning scattered notes into study guides, taking complicated PDFs and making them simple, breaking down concepts into steps I could follow. Saturn is the reason I can move from confusion to clarity, because I learned that structure isn’t limiting; it frees me to do more. Each ring is a routine, a strategy, or a way of thinking I built to keep myself balanced.


Uranus

This planet fits the moments when I questioned normal approaches, especially when writing essays or analyzing how classrooms work, how objectivity fails students, or how small acts of rebellion reshape society. It’s the part of my journey where creativity met logic and I realized STEM isn’t just numbers, it’s about perspective. Uranus reminds me that sometimes I have to rotate at a strange angle to see things clearly.


Neptune

Neptune reflects the moments when I felt overwhelmed, when problems stretched out like an the vast ice planet and I wasn’t sure if I had the skills to swim through them. When I questioned whether I understood something well enough, or whether I was improving at all, Neptune is where I drifted. But it’s also where I learned one of the most important lessons: it’s okay to not know everything. Curiosity matters more than certainty.

Pluto

Whether it was a single math question, a simple turtle function, a misunderstanding about logs, or refining a single sentence in an essay, Pluto represents the tiny steps that didn’t seem significant at the time. Even when something felt “too small” to count, it added up. Pluto is where I learned that progress isn’t always measured in big leaps

The STEM Journey is like our great Solar System. Vast of ideas, knowledge, yet full of emptiness and even lonliness. But within the infinite void of space, there exists planets, each one being so different then the last. Within these planets are the steps of the journey, always something new. Exciting or fearful, Nervousness or Excitement, it's one big solar system, highs and lows alike.
